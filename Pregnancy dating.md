- Our aim when dating a pregnancy is
	- to get the **EDD** (expected date of delivery)
		- Menstrual dating: getting the EDD from LMP
		- Ultrasound dating: getting EDD from US using fetal biometry
	- to calculate the current **GA** (conveniently from EDD, for easy comparison with US regardless of its GA)
- Are there different types of “ages” for a pregnancy?
	- Menstrual age = **GA**: from the LMP
	- Conception age: from fertilization = menstrual -- 2 weeks
	- Implantation age: from implantation = menstrual -- 3 weeks
- What is the EDD?
	- When calculating GA from an average 28-day cycle, EDD = 280 days (40 weeks) after LMP
		- For cycles longer than 28 days: add the difference from 28
		- For cycles shorter than 28 days: subtract the difference from 28
	- To get the EDD from the LMP (the 280 d = 40 w), we apply **Naegele rule**:
	  $$EDD = LMP + 7d - 3m + 1y$$
		**Tip**: if LMP \< April → $EDD = LMP + 7d + 9m$
- Menstrual dating should be confirmed with US dating before 20 weeks.\
  Otherwise, this pregnancy is *suboptimally dated.*
	- EDD can be reassigned only once (if any) if the discrepancy threshold is met, according to the GA at US.
